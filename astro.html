Astrophotography

So far, I've only done wide-field astrophotography although I am interested in some deep-sky imaging in the future. I currently use my Canon EOS M50 along with a Sigma 16mm F1.4 lense in order to capture more light (generally sticking around 20-25 second long shutter speed and an ISO of around 1000). I also used Sequator for the first time and I can definitely notice an improvement in quality and a large reduction of noise in the images. I am still learning different techniques in order to improve the results. I am also dabbling a bit in GIMP (since it is free). The photos imported into Sequator need to be RAW (in order to remove hot pixels), which means post processing is rather necessary, or else you get a less than satisfying result. I will hopefully have more opportunities to go dark sky places and do some more astrophotography.
